Appraising Walnut Creek's Established Market

Walnut Creek is one of Contra Costa County's most active real estate markets — and one of the most complex to appraise accurately. The city encompasses everything from condominium complexes near BART to custom hillside estates in the Northgate and Tice Valley neighborhoods, and pricing these properties requires genuine local market knowledge.

The market's diversity is its defining characteristic. A one-bedroom condo in downtown Walnut Creek and a four-bedroom home on the hillside above Tice Valley require completely different comparable selection and adjustment methodology. Brandi Hudson (#AR029078) has 25 years of certified appraisal experience throughout Contra Costa County and understands these distinctions.

What Makes Walnut Creek Properties Unique to Appraise

  • BART proximity premiums — Properties within walking distance of the Walnut Creek BART station command premiums that require careful market support.
  • Downtown condominium market — The downtown condo market has its own dynamics, HOA fee impacts, and comparable selection challenges distinct from single-family homes.
  • Hillside and view properties — Northgate and Tice Valley hillside homes with Mt. Diablo views require view adjustment methodology specific to this market.
  • Lamorinda spillover — The Lafayette and Orinda border areas create interesting comparable selection questions that require local knowledge to navigate.

Frequently Asked Questions — Walnut Creek Appraisals

Do you appraise condominiums in downtown Walnut Creek?

Yes. Condominium appraisals require specific methodology including HOA fee analysis, project-level review, and careful comparable selection within the same or similar complexes. We have experience with Walnut Creek's downtown condominium market.

How do BART proximity premiums affect Walnut Creek appraisals?

BART proximity can add measurable value in Walnut Creek, particularly for buyers who commute to San Francisco. We use paired sales analysis to support these adjustments with verified market data rather than applying a standard percentage.
